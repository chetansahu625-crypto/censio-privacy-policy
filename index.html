Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 12, 2026

Censio ("the app") is a population and demographics data viewer. This policy explains what data Censio does — and does not — collect.

Summary

Censio does not collect, store, or share any personal data. There are no user accounts, no analytics, no tracking, and no advertising identifiers in this app.

What data we collect

None. Censio does not ask you to create an account, does not request any personal information, and does not include any analytics or crash-reporting SDKs that would collect data about you or your device.

What the app does

Censio displays publicly available population and demographic statistics (sourced from the United Nations Population Division and the World Bank) by making read-only requests to our own backend API. These requests retrieve country and demographic data only — they do not include any information that identifies you personally.

Third-party services

Censio's backend is hosted on Render and stores data in Supabase. Neither service receives any personal data from you through this app, since the app itself doesn't collect any. Standard, non-identifying network-level information (such as IP address) may be visible to these infrastructure providers as an inherent part of any internet request, the same as visiting any website — this is not used by Censio for tracking or profiling.

Children's privacy

Censio does not knowingly collect data from anyone, including children, because it does not collect data from anyone at all.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes — for example, if a future version of the app adds a feature that does collect data — this page will be updated and the "Last updated" date above will reflect the change.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be sent to censio.support@gmail.com.
